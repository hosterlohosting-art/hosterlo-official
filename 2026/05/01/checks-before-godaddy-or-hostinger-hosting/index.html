Top 10 Checks Before Choosing GoDaddy or Hostinger Hosting

May 1, 20267 min readHosterlo Team

GoDaddy and Hostinger are two of the most popular hosting providers, but are they the right choice for your website? Before you commit, run through these 10 critical checks.

1. Check the Renewal Price

Both GoDaddy and Hostinger advertise low introductory prices, but renewal rates can be 2—3x higher. Always check what you'll pay after the first term.

2. Read the Uptime Guarantee

Look for a 99.9% uptime guarantee backed by an SLA. Some budget hosts offer no uptime guarantee at all.

3. Verify Storage Type

NVMe SSD is the fastest storage technology available. Some hosts still use traditional HDDs, which are significantly slower.

4. Check for Free SSL

SSL is essential for security and SEO. Make sure it's included free – some hosts charge extra.

5. Evaluate Support Quality

Test the support chat before buying. Ask a technical question and see how fast and helpful the response is.

6. Look at Backup Frequency

Daily backups should be standard. Weekly or manual-only backups put your data at risk.

7. Check Email Hosting

Does the plan include email? How many accounts? Some hosts limit or charge extra for business email.

8. Verify Migration Support

If you're switching hosts, free migration saves time and headaches. Check if it's included.

9. Review the Control Panel

cPanel is the industry standard. Some hosts use proprietary panels that are harder to use or lack features.

10. Compare the Total Package

Don't just compare price – compare what you get. A host that includes free domain, SSL, backups, email, and cPanel at $4.99/mo is better value than one at $2.99/mo with add-on charges. If you are building a WordPress site, check if they offer optimized WordPress hosting plans with automated updates and speed caching built in.
